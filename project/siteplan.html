Buka & Beyond

Website Planning Document

1. Site Name

Name: Buka & Beyond

Logo Concept: A stylized, modern cooking clay pot (Ape) with a clean, elegant spoon sweeping upwards to symbolize modern culinary discovery.

Reason for Selection: This name honors local Nigerian bukas (traditional food joints) while signaling that the platform goes "beyond" to explore the deep histories, vibrant cultures, and specific locations of authentic local street dining.

2. Site Purpose

Buka & Beyond serves as an interactive cultural hub and restaurant discovery platform for local food enthusiasts and tourists. The site maps regional bukhas, details their signature cultural dishes (such as Amala, Pounded Yam, or Jollof variants), and offers historical backgrounds on these culinary traditions. It also provides an interactive portal with submission forms where users can suggest or review neighborhood bukhas to support local businesses.

3. Target Audience Scenarios

  • Scenario A: Where can I find the highest-rated, authentic Amala spot in the Surulere or Ikeja areas that includes secure local parking?
  • Scenario B: How do I register or recommend my neighborhood's favorite hidden roadside buka so it can get listed on this platform?

4. Color Schema

This planning document directly utilizes the project's selected color palette:

Primary Color #8B263E (Deep Pepper Red) Used for: Headers, primary branding elements, and section roots.
Secondary Color #D4A373 (Warm Clay Gold) Used for: Call-to-action details, borders, and visual accents.
Background Light #FFFDF9 (Off-White / Rice Cream) Used for: Clean, readable background canvasses across pages.

5. Typography

Cabinet Grotesk (Bold Sanskrit/Display Sans)

Applied to: All main website headers, page block titles, and primary logo branding text.

Satoshi (Clean Geometric Sans)

Applied to: Standard body text, descriptive review boxes, paragraphs, forms, and footer metrics.
